abstract: "LiveCells is a 2D pixel art samurai action-platformer built in Godot 4, built around a CombatEntity base class that centralizes hitbox activation, health management, and particle spawning into a unified signal-based system shared by both the player and all enemies. The player executes a 3-hit combo, a frame-perfect parry, and a dash with invincibility frames across multi-stage levels with wall-jump traversal. Four enemy archetypes impose distinct adaptive demands: Shoto (balanced approach-defense mix), Rushdown (aggressive lunge pressure), Zoner (ranged triple-shot at maintained distance), and Grappler (slow, high-damage punisher). Profile-based hitboxes define attack properties — position, size, and damage — per animation frame. A loot economy distributes 14 food items with varying health restoration values plus a penalty item. Hit-stop, directional sparks, and a 32-stream pooled AudioManager provide layered combat feedback. Singleton managers govern SFX pooling, loot state and global hit-stop, and top-level game state transitions."
